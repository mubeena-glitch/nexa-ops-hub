Website Design SOP

10-Step Process · Brief to Handoff
Scope: This SOP applies to all new website and full redesign projects at NEXA. Every designer must follow this process on every project regardless of platform or size.
End-to-End Design Process
From brief receipt to approved developer handoff
▲
01
Brief Review
Receive brief from PM. Read all 11 mandatory fields. Review brand assets, competitor references and platform requirements. Flag any gaps to PM within 4 hours of brief receipt. Do not begin design until brief is confirmed complete.
02
Research & Direction
Review client's brand, market position, competitors and inspiration provided. Define the design direction in a brief written note (2–3 sentences) shared with PM before design begins. Reference at least 3 competitor or inspiration sites.
03
Two Homepage Concepts (Mandatory)
Design two distinct full-fidelity homepage concept directions. Concept A: primary direction aligned to brief. Concept B: alternative creative interpretation. Both must be complete designs at desktop breakpoint — no wireframes, no partial layouts presented as concepts. Single-concept submissions are not permitted without written PM and Partner approval.
04
Internal Review (PM must approve before client sees)
Share both concepts with PM for internal review. PM reviews for brand alignment, quality standards and client expectations. Apply all PM feedback before client presentation. Never share first-pass designs directly with the client — this is a non-negotiable quality gate.
05
Client Presentation
PM leads the client presentation. Designer joins the call if required. Present both concepts with a brief rationale for each direction. Do not present a personal preference — present both objectively. All client feedback logged in Forecast by PM during or immediately after the meeting. Designer does not log feedback directly.
06
Revision Rounds (Maximum 2 rounds in standard scope)
Apply client feedback from Forecast. Up to 2 client revision rounds are included in standard project scope. If the client requests a third round, PM raises a change request with the Partner before the designer continues any additional work. Requests to change the concept direction after approval are a change request, not a revision round.
07
Written Client Approval on Chosen Concept
Written client approval on the chosen concept direction must be received before any inner pages begin or handoff to developer. Approval must be in writing — email confirmation or Forecast comment. Verbal approval is not accepted. PM owns this approval step.
08
Inner Pages
Design all inner pages consistent with the approved homepage direction. Maintain visual consistency across typography, spacing, colour usage and component patterns. Every page must be delivered at desktop (1440px), tablet (768px) and mobile (375px) breakpoints. No inner page is handoff-ready until all three breakpoints are complete.
09
Responsive Adaptations
Complete all responsive designs. Navigation, forms, images, accordions and interactive elements must be shown correctly at all breakpoints. Check that no content is cut off, overlapping or missing at tablet or mobile sizes. Run a WCAG AA colour contrast check on all body text and CTA combinations before handoff.
10
Handoff to Developer
Complete the Design → Developer Handover Checklist 100% before notifying the developer. Organise the Figma file into pages: Homepage / Inner Pages / Components / Assets. Export all assets and upload to the project portal. Share the Figma link with view access confirmed. Notify PM in Forecast that handoff is complete.
AI Target: Use AI tools (Figma AI, Midjourney or equivalent) in at least 30% of projects. Document AI usage in Forecast for quarterly KPI review.
HubSpot Design Rules
Mandatory for all HubSpot projects
▼
  • All designs must use a modular section structure — each section is a self-contained, independently editable unit. No full-page monolithic layouts.
  • Module names in Figma must match the naming convention agreed with the development team before design begins.
  • All editable fields (text, images, CTAs, buttons) must be highlighted and annotated in the handoff Figma file.
  • No fixed-width elements that cannot be adapted within HubSpot's responsive grid.
  • Designs must account for HubSpot's native menu, form, blog and membership module constraints — do not design elements that HubSpot cannot render natively.
  • All CTA buttons must follow the standard hover and active state specification agreed with the developer.
Quality Completion Criteria
All items required before handoff
▼
  • Both homepage concepts delivered at full fidelity
  • Written client approval received on chosen concept
  • All inner pages designed at all three breakpoints (1440 / 768 / 375)
  • Design → Developer Handover Checklist 100% complete
  • Figma file organised and shared with developer — view access confirmed
  • All assets exported and uploaded to project portal
  • All brand colours defined as Figma styles — no hardcoded hex values
  • PM notified of handoff completion in Forecast

Website Development SOP

12-Step Process · Handoff to Go-Live
Scope: This SOP applies to all new website builds and full rebuilds at NEXA. Applies to all platforms — HubSpot, WordPress and custom builds — on every project without exception.
End-to-End Development Process
From design handoff to post-launch warranty
▲
01
Handoff Review
Review the Design → Developer Handover Checklist with the designer. Confirm Figma access, all assets available, all copy final, platform confirmed and approval gate signed. Flag any missing items, unclear specs or platform conflicts to PM before starting any build work. Do not begin building until all handoff items are resolved.
02
Staging Environment Setup
HubSpot: use sandbox or cloned portal — never build on the live portal. WordPress: set up a staging subdomain. Custom builds: use a local development environment or staging server. Confirm staging environment is working before any code is written. Establish Git version control from day one on all custom and WordPress builds.
03
Platform & Environment Setup
Install and configure all platform dependencies: theme, plugins, HubSpot modules or framework as agreed with PM. Configure hosting environment if new. For WordPress: install only approved plugins reviewed for security and performance. Pin all WordPress core and plugin versions before build begins. No off-the-shelf themes unless explicitly scoped.
04
Homepage Build (First)
Build the homepage first — it is the design reference for all inner pages. Match the approved Figma design pixel-accurately at all three breakpoints (desktop, tablet, mobile). Complete a thorough self-QA against the design before submitting to PM for review. Measure PageSpeed after homepage is complete — flag immediately if score is below target.
05
Inner Pages (After Homepage PM Approval)
Only begin inner pages after PM has approved the homepage build. Reuse components and modules wherever possible — avoid rebuilding the same element from scratch per page. Log time against each page in Forecast daily. For HubSpot: use the same module structure as the homepage. For WordPress: use the same ACF field structure.
06
Integrations Setup & Testing
Set up all integrations specified in the brief: CRM form mapping, Google Analytics 4, Google Tag Manager, HubSpot tracking, Meta Pixel, LinkedIn Insight Tag, live chat, email platform connections. Test every integration flow end-to-end on staging before submitting for QA. Every form submission must arrive in the correct CRM destination with correct field mapping before QA is marked complete.
07
Developer QA — 100% Checklist Required
Complete the full Developer QA Checklist before submitting anything to PM. This covers: responsiveness at all breakpoints, cross-browser testing (Chrome, Firefox, Safari, Edge, iOS Safari, Android Chrome), PageSpeed scores, Core Web Vitals, all integration flows, all forms, all links, SEO basics (meta titles, H1 structure, alt text, sitemap, canonical tags), and accessibility basics. Do not submit to PM until every item is checked and resolved.
08
PM Review
PM reviews the build against the approved Figma design and the project brief. Developer receives PM feedback via Forecast. Address all PM feedback within the agreed timeframe — log every fix in Forecast. Do not push any fixes directly to staging without logging them. If a fix raises a new issue, flag to PM before proceeding.
09
Client Review on Staging
PM shares staging link with client and facilitates the review. Developer addresses all client feedback logged in Forecast by PM. Up to 2 client review rounds are included in standard scope. If a third round is requested, PM raises a change request with the Partner before the developer continues any additional work.
10
Pre-Launch Gate — Full Checklist Required
Complete the full Pre-Launch Checklist before any go-live is scheduled. Confirm the go-live is on a Monday–Thursday. Obtain written PM sign-off before making any changes to the live environment. Never go live without written PM approval — this is non-negotiable regardless of time pressure from client.
11
Go-Live Execution

UI/UX Audit SOP

12-Step Audit · Research to Deliverable
Scope: A NEXA UI/UX audit evaluates an existing website's usability, visual design consistency, conversion performance, mobile experience, technical performance and accessibility. Output is a structured report with prioritised, actionable recommendations.
12-Step Audit Process
Client questionnaire to report delivery
▲
01
Client Questionnaire
Send the NEXA audit questionnaire to the client. Collect: business goals, target audience, top-performing pages, known pain points, analytics access confirmation, primary conversion goals and any previous audit findings. Do not begin audit work until the questionnaire is returned.
02
Analytics Review
Access GA4 (or equivalent analytics platform). Review: top pages by traffic, bounce rate by page, average session duration, conversion funnel drop-off points, device split (desktop/tablet/mobile), top exit pages and any goal or event data available. Document key data points that will inform the audit findings.
03
Heuristic Evaluation
Evaluate the site against Nielsen's 10 Usability Heuristics: Visibility of system status, Match between system and the real world, User control and freedom, Consistency and standards, Error prevention, Recognition rather than recall, Flexibility and efficiency of use, Aesthetic and minimalist design, Error recovery, Help and documentation. Document findings with annotated screenshots for each heuristic where issues are found.
04
UI Consistency Audit
Review typography usage (font sizes, weights, hierarchy consistency), colour usage (brand compliance, contrast ratios), spacing and layout rhythm, button and CTA styles across pages, form design consistency, icon style and sizing, and overall visual consistency between pages. Document all inconsistencies with annotated screenshots.
05
Conversion Audit
Review all CTAs, forms and conversion paths across the site. Assess: CTA placement and visibility, CTA copy and clarity, button contrast and size, form length and friction, trust signals and social proof placement, value proposition clarity, and any conversion path friction. Compare against conversion rate optimisation best practices.
06
Mobile Review
Test on iOS Safari (iPhone 12 minimum) and Android Chrome at 375px and 768px. Document: layout breaks or overflow, text that is too small to read without zooming, tap targets smaller than 44×44px, interstitial or popup issues on mobile, slow load times on mobile network, and any functionality that does not work on touch devices.
07
Technical Performance
Run PageSpeed Insights for both desktop and mobile on the top 5 pages by traffic. Document all Core Web Vitals scores (LCP, CLS, FID/INP) and identify the top 3 performance issues per page. Note render-blocking resources, unoptimised images, unused JavaScript and any third-party script performance impact.
08
Accessibility Review
Test against WCAG 2.1 AA criteria: colour contrast ratios on all body text and interactive elements, alt text on all images, keyboard navigation through the site, form field labels and error messaging, focus states on all interactive elements, and ARIA roles where applicable. Use automated tools (axe, Lighthouse) plus manual keyboard navigation testing.
09
Competitor Benchmarking
Review 2–3 competitor or industry-leading websites relevant to the client. Document 3–5 specific design or UX patterns that outperform the client's current site. Focus on features that are directly achievable and relevant — not just "they have a better brand."
10
Report Compilation
Compile all findings into the NEXA Audit Report template. Every finding must be: categorised by priority (Critical / High / Medium / Low), accompanied by a screenshot, and followed by a specific, actionable recommendation. Vague findings without recommendations are not acceptable in the final report.
11
PM Internal Review
PM reviews the completed report for completeness, clarity and professional presentation before client delivery. Designer reviews the UI Consistency and Heuristic sections for accuracy. Any finding that cannot be substantiated with a screenshot or data must be removed or revised.
12
Client Delivery
PM or AM presents the report to the client in a structured meeting. Walk through all Critical and High findings with the client — do not just send the PDF without a presentation. Provide the full report PDF as a leave-behind. Follow up within 5 business days to scope any recommended work.
Priority Classification
▼
🔴 Critical
Broken functionality, major accessibility failure, or issue directly preventing conversions. Must be fixed before any other work.
🟠 High
Significant UX or performance issue on a high-traffic page or primary conversion path. Fix in the next sprint.
🔵 Medium
Design inconsistency or UX improvement with meaningful impact but not immediately blocking.
🟣 Low
Minor cosmetic or enhancement suggestion. Implement when capacity allows.
Report Structure
▼
1. Executive Summary3–5 sentence overview of scope, key findings and priority action
2. Heuristic EvaluationFindings mapped to Nielsen's 10 heuristics with screenshots
3. UI ConsistencyTypography, colour, spacing and component consistency findings
4. Conversion AnalysisCTA, form and conversion path assessment with recommendations
5. Mobile ExperienceDevice-specific findings with annotated screenshots
6. Technical PerformancePageSpeed scores, Core Web Vitals, top optimisation opportunities
7. AccessibilityWCAG AA compliance gaps with specific elements identified
8. Competitor Benchmarking3 benchmark comparisons with lessons for the client
9. Priority MatrixAll findings mapped to Critical / High / Medium / Low grid
10. Recommended Next Steps3–5 specific, actionable recommendations with implementation order
